I was part of organisers a local event in Northampton known as Campfire Northampton! This was part of Hack Club's Campfire Event which is a student-led event which ran in over 200 cities on the same date of 28th of February 2026. They provided a lot of the resources to help us run the event such as the prize money, t-shirts, organiser guide to show us how to organise such event showing how to get a venue and contact sponsors, safeguard training and more!

The event ended up being a huge success as there was 22 sign-ups with 21 people actually coming to the event which is huge for such an event as before the event I never really thought Northampton had much teens that enjoyed coding. Even better is that out of the 21 people who turned up 21 shipped giving us a ship rate of 100% which compared to other Campfire's was amazing.

We also ran after the event a feedback form to ask all the attendees how good the event was according to them and using the results it was calculated as an NPS Score (Net Promoter Score) which ours was a 75. This is from a range of -100 to 100 which is insanely good for such an event meaning most of the attendees who came found that the event was amazing and while asking at the event if they would come to such event again a lot said yes. This is amazing as it shows how much teens actually enjoy coding and we might do another event in the future. The NPS Score also meant our organiser team is now part of TOP (Trusted Organiser Programme) in HackClub meaning for the next event if it takes place we will get more money to run the event.

In the months leading up to the event it was a lot of work behind the scenes just to get the event to actually happen, we managed to secure Growing Together in Northampton as a venue for the event which was an awesome venue for this but we also managed to secure a sponsorship with Yubico which gave us 40 YubiKey's part of their Secure It Forward programme. This was our first time running such an event so we had to learn how to advertise the event, fundraise to afford such an event, figure out communication with the attendees, complete a risk assessment and organise supervision for safety at the event.

All this work lead up to the day of the event, which did have its issues as what you would expect but it still turned out to be a great event. We had an opening ceremony which technical issues arose with sound for the theme video and the device running the presentation being a bit slow (probably wasn't the smartest idea to run the presentation on a device booting over a USB with 2.0 speeds running an Ubuntu live image). We still managed to power through the issues and managed to finish the opening ceremony slightly later than we expected with the end being around 15 minutes later than what was planned in the schedule.

Now the participants got into groups and was set off to work on their games! Some participants knew how to code with some making stuff in unity while others was completely new to game development and we became prepared for that. We had the training from Campfire Overdrive on how to make games in GameMaker (I made [Quacky Duck](https://gizzywantsheadpats.itch.io/quacky-duck) for it) but HackClub also made a participant guide to show how to make games in [GameMaker](https://tutorial.campfire.hackclub.com/). Not much happened after that just us organisers walking through the rooms helping people out with issues in their games while they was making it and helping them with ideas of their games. We served lunch at 1:00 which was a variety of Subway Sandwiches and some fruit with Coke Zero, Juice, and Water for people to drink. After lunch all participants continued to work on their games.

At 5:00pm we then asked participants to get at least something published to itch.io so there was something we can use at the time of voting. Glad we did do this as there was issues with unity struggling to export to a web project to upload to itch.io. We also allowed participants to still make tweaks to their games until 6:00pm just wanted a game that worked ready for voting if they didn't finish in time. Although we were slightly late to voting because of the exporting issues pushing voting back a bit I don't know the exact time we started voting. We were originally going to use the projector to show off people's games on a big screen but we had more technical issues! The projector stopped working for some reason refusing to show an image so we had to get people to go around to each others dividing sides of rooms so one side showed other side their games and then swap that around for the other side to show the original side their games. 

On to voting we go! Oh wait we just had to have more technical issues! Yeah this was slightly annoying we got participants to go onto [Podium](https://podium.hackclub.com) to go vote on the projects they liked the most out of the choices of Blue Current, Falling into Heaven, Flap, Agartha, The Great Discape, Moles Last Stand and Bug Escape. When we tried we got let known by participants that the vote button was disabled, they were unable to vote so we had to quickly improvise. We pulled out a piece of paper made participants get into a line and wrote down the votes manually. Not the best or secret way to do it but what could we actually do? We had to figure out something quickly as the end was quickly approaching. The projector was still broken so we couldn't do the close ceremony presentation so we manually said who won. It was Blue Current! Congrats to them for winning. They won the prize of Blahaj's for each one in their team. We also gave away the small campfires we got for the event to the team with the best name which was Agartha!

This was the time everyone started leaving, we had a stand at the door giving out the t-shirts given to us for the Campfire event by HackClub and the Yubikey's given to us by Yubico's Secure It Forward programee because everyone worked hard on the day so they deserved to get something from the event too. This comes to the end of Campfire we started to clean up the venue packing stuff away and said thanks to the caretakers of the venue for allowing our venue to take place there. Even tried to offer one a YubiKey but they declined because they'd probably lose it and lose access to their accounts.

This event was amazing and first hackathon event for me. I enjoyed organising it so much and the technical errors added character to the event. We are only human, we couldn't see any of these issues coming and nor could HackClub as it was 200+ Cities who also had to use the same system and a lot was voting at the same time that is a lot of load on the system. Hopefully for future events its better equipped for such load and works.
